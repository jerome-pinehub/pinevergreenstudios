This year, the theme of the half-day meeting was continental collaboration. Drupa!
DP goes to Düsseldorf

 

It’s big. Really, really big. So much so, in fact, that just the catalogue clocks in at nearly 900 pages and is printed in 10 different languages. What is it? Why, Drupa, of course!

Billed as the largest industry trade show in the world, Drupa brings hundreds of thousands of visitors to Düsseldorf every four years who come to see what’s now and what’s next in the printing industry. According to Wikipedia, “394,332 visitors attended Drupa in 2004, and 1,866 exhibitors from 52 countries covered a total of 16.1 hectares (39.8 acres) of space.”

Jim’s Corner

by Jim Longnecker

It’s Autumn, and that makes me think of cider, apples, and colorful leaves. Of course, here in Document Publishing, color isn’t limited only to the leaves! DP is breaking new ground with the Color Management Initiative, well into Phase I. David Richter, Suk Kim, and Ian Mutton are heading up the team working with representatives from Print and Mail, Corporate Creative, MPC, and Statement Production to ensure consistent output across the globe by applying Gracol 7 print standards to the newly approved T. Rowe Price corporate color palette. Managing color across output devices both internally and externally plays a significant role in managing the T. Rowe Price brand.The team spent countless hours refining and proofing the colors and will soon launch a swatch book. Many thanks to the team and all who have helped move the Color Management Initiative forward.

 

Speaking of moving forward, the 2009 Planning process is complete except for the prioritization of certain initiatives. The managers and supervisors will meet in the next few weeks to access feedback from our business partners and incorporate them into the Document Publishing 2009 Plan. I currently don’t anticipate any major changes, but do expect that the timing of several of the smaller initiatives may be extended due to market conditions and business direction. The final plan will be shared with all DP associates upon completion.
